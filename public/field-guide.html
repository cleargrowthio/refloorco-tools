Rep field guide
Scripts — word for word
Qualifying the lead
"Is it one specific thing like flooring or paint, or are you thinking about a bigger renovation?"
Verbal prelim (D&B — on the call)
"Based on what you're describing, you're probably looking somewhere in the range of [low] to [high] depending on the level of finish. Does that range work for your budget?"
Sending the prelim document
"Let me send you a written breakdown so you have something to reference. I'll get that over in the next hour — once you've had a chance to look it over, just reply and we'll get a walkthrough on the calendar."
Presenting the price (productized)
"Based on [X] sq ft, you're looking at [price] for the [service]. That includes [key inclusions]. The 50% deposit to lock in your start date would be [deposit amount]."
Asking for the deposit
"To get you on the schedule, I just need the 50% deposit today — that's [amount]. We can do card, check, or Zelle. Want to go ahead and lock in your date?"
Customer says 'I need to think about it'
"Totally understand. I'll leave this with you. Our schedule fills up fast — if you want to hold a spot, even just the deposit gets you in line. I'll follow up tomorrow at [time]."
Presenting D&B tiers
"We have three renovation levels. Refresh is cosmetic — floors, paint, cabinets, fixtures. Renovate adds countertops, vanities, doors, exterior. Transform is a full gut we design custom for your layout. Based on what you showed me, I think [tier] is the right fit — but look at all three."
Referral + review ask
"Really glad it came out the way you wanted. If you know anyone thinking about a renovation, we'd love the referral. I'll send you a Google review link right now."
